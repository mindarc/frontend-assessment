Explain why the result of
`('b' + 'a' + + 'a' + 'a').toLowerCase()`
is `banana`.

+'a' resolves to NaN ("Not a Number") because it coerces a string to be a number, while the character a cannot be parsed as a number. And when converted to lowercase it becomes banana. Adding NaN to "ba" turns NaN into the string "NaN" due to type conversion, gives baNaN. And then there is an a behind, giving baNaNa.

From Javascript:
document.getElementById("banana-js").innerHTML = (("b"+"a"+ +"a"+"a").toLowerCase());
